Gameplay

Candy Crush Saga is a "match three" game, where the core gameplay is
based on swapping two adjecent candies among several on the gameboard
as to make a row or column at least three matching -colored candies. On
this match, the matched candies are removed from the board, and candies
above them fall into the empty spaces, with new candies appearing form
the top of the board. This may creat a new matched set of candies, which
is automatically cleared in the same manner. The player scores points for
these matches and gains progressively more pionts for chain reaction.
Additionally, creating matches of four or more candies will creat special
candy that, when matched, can clear row, column, or other section of the
board.

The game is split among many levels, which must be completed in sequence. Each level poses a different challenge to the user, such as achieving a
minimum score in a fixed number of moves or clearing candies in fixed number of moves to bring special ingredients to the bottom of the board.
Board have a number of different configurations and may include special spaces taht have thier own unique rules, such as space covered with jelly
that must be cleared by making a match on that space. If the player meets the level's goal, they will be given from one to three stars based on their
score and can proceed into the next level. Otherwise, they will lose one life and must try again. If the player run out of lives, they have to wait for
some period of real-world time while their lives regenerate before attempting the level again.Completed levels can be replayed if desired.

In-App Purchases

The game is primarily monetized through in-app Purchases (through either a credit card, Itunes crdits or Google Play credits); Players begin with
five "lives", lost whenever a level is failed. This applies all of King's games. When they are exausted, users can either send requests to their facebook
friends for more lives, wait for them to replenish themselves (a life is restored evry half-hour), or Purchase them. Initially when a player makes
for a new lives, they receive five new lives for a specific dollar ammount. In a recent update, when a player makes a purchase for a new lives, the
player receives gold bars, the quantity depending on how much money they spend. Gold bars can be used for new lives, extra moves, boosters or
to unlock a new episode. At certain points, primarily at the start of nwe "episodes", users must also either purchase or receive a request from at
least three friends before they may access the next set of level. An update meant players waited for only three days to unlock the next level.

Booster, to make the level easier, can be bought using in-app purchases. While the game includes freemium content, 97.7% of those playing
the game do so for free, while only 2.3% pay.

Development

Prior to the release of Candy Crush Saga, most of king's games were browser games offered through their website or the partner portals such
as Yahoo!. This include Candy Crush, a straightforward tile-matching game released in 2011 which King's cheif creative officer and co-founder
Sebastain Knutsson said come after few hundred of other games thae had designed for the portal. Candy Crush's cancept had been based on an
early game King made, Miner Speed, that borrowed gameplay elements from Bejeweled. Candy Crush added new animations for the candies,
and expressive paper doll- like Characters that helped to make the game one of five most popular ones on the site by 2012.

Around 2009, Facebook began to pull in developers, in purticular Zynga, to offer socail network games that coud be build on its fundamental
services; for king, this resulting in a large drop in players that they saw from their game portals within a year. At this point, king started to
determine how it could enter the facebook and the associated mobile game market, breaking up its web development department to work on
facebook and mobile games in 2010, including bringing several of their existing browser games to those platform.

In April 2011, King released its existing portal game Miner Speed as it first cross-platform (facebook and mobile) game to figure out the
transition between facebook and mobile games for this new direction. King's first major success in this area followed eith Bubble Witch Saga,
released in October 2011; by January 2012 it has attracted over 10 million players and was one of fastest rising facebook game at that time.
Bubble Switch Saga introduced the "saga" approach in contrast to typical tile-matching games, where intead of having game continue through
a fixed ammount of time or untill the player reached an unplayable state, the game was divided into discrete levels that required the player to
complete certain goals within a fixed set of moves, and where the next level could only be reached after completing the previous level.

King later released mobile version for IOS and Anroid that same year, adding a feature that allowed mobile users to synchronize their progress
with the facebook version. Knutsson started that at that time, with Candy Crush Saga as popular as it was on facebook, they knew that they
"had to get it right" in the transition process. King had previously discussed the nature of the games that kept their state between a PC and
mobile version with fabrication games, believing this was necessary trend in the future of the gaming, both recognized several of the difficulties
that would have to be addressed to provide both the progress synchronization and gameplay interface between mouse-driven PC computers and
touch-driven mobile devices.
