   Tennis Elbow (VEDIO GAME)

  Tennis Elbow is a series of tennis vedio games developed by France. Its first version was released in 1996 and as of
  May 2005 it is currrntly on its 2013 version, available for Windows, Mac & Linux platforms.

  The game is sumulation oriented, and is often seen as being the one offering the most realistic tennis experience in
  term of rallies and tannis sensations.

  The most noticeable particularity of its gameplay is that the users have to hold down the strike button till thier player
  actually strikes the ball,unlike most other tennis games that require the user to release the strike button earlier.

  After more than 2 years spent in steam Greenlight, the 2013 version finally got released on Stream on March 13,2005.

  The Game is regularly updated and as of July 2018 has senn 10 more updates for the 2013 edition.

  The next edition is called "Tennis Elbow 4" and its early version was planned for 2019. It was released on the 4th
  of the June,2021,and is still in ealry access as the 10th of March 2022.

   Tenns Elbow (1997)

  The first release in this tennis series allows players to compete in a World Tour of 90 events against 250 players.
  Single match can be dirty up and dirty down (two against one) as well as the more coventional singles and doubles.
  Grass, clay, flexi and cement courts are featured. Action replays can be viewed in slow motion, fast speed and rewound.

  Players can be defined as volleyers, defenders, punchers or varied, with definable kit colours and playing abilities. As
  you play the game , yours players ability is improves, resulting in faster and more accurate serves as well as faster running.

  Tennis Elbow 2004 is an enhanced windows port of the orignal Tennis Elbow.

   Tennis Elbow 2006(2006)

  Tennis Elbow 2006 allows palyers to compete in a World Tour of 125 events against 300 players. Both the entry and the champions'Race
  ranking systems are incorporated-the former covering the last 52 weeks and the letter being fixed over a single callendar yet.

  Single matches can be two against one, as well as more conventional singles and doubles. Network and internet play is offered. There are
  seven distinct court types-clay, grass, hard, blue-green hard, synthetic, indoor hard and indoor synthetic. Action replays can be veiwed in
  slow motion, fast speed and rewound.

  Players can be defined as volleyers, defenders, punchers or varied, with definable kit colours and playing abilities. As you play the game,
  your palyer's abilities improves- by more if you lose. Assign these points for faster and more accurate serves as well as faster running and
  bigger jumps.

   Tennis Elbow Manager (2009)

  In this tennis manager game, you take control of a tennis coach and his palyer, and your goal is to reach Number one ranking in the World,
  to stay thier as long as possible and win as many Grand Slam titles as possible.

● Men or Women Tour
● 3500 players evolving over 38 years, from 1990 to 2027
● More than 300 tournaments
● World Team Cup and Masters Cup
● Singles & doubles
● Up to 4 coached players
● 5 difficulty levels
● Full ranking systems (like the entry one from ATP)
● In game languages: English, French, Deutsch, Croatian, Italian, Dutch


  The game was Greenlit on Stream and later released on October 14, 2016

   Tennis Elbow 2013(2013)

  Tennis Elbow 2013 follows the previous instalments in the Tennis Elobow series. It is described as an updated version of the previous tittle
  Tennis Elbow 2011 and it is provide for free to owners of that game. It features a simulative approch to tennis, and offer, along with local
  and online multiplayer, three single player modes: training, single match and World Tour, a career mode.

  Gameplay can be tweaked to more or less simulative through option such as turning fatigue on or off, having the indicators of where our shots
  and the opponent's will impact on the court displayed or not, different CPU levels (6 in total,from "beginner" to "incredible"), and choosing from
  Arcade, Simulation, Elite "controls", which impacts the gameplay, making it easier or harder to do good shots.

  The game was Greenlit on stream and later released on March 13, 2015.
