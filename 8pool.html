   EIGHT-BALL

  Eight-ball is a pool billiards played on a billiard table with six pockets, cue sticks, and sixteen
  billiered balls numbered 9 through 15, and the black 8 ball. After the balls are scattered with a
  break shot, a player assigned either the goup of solid or striped balls once they have legally
  pocketed a ball from that group. the object of the game is to legally pocket the 8-ball in a pocket,
  which can only be done after all of the balls from player assigned group have been cleared
  from the table.


  The game has numreous variation, it is the second most played proffestional pool game, after
  nine-ball.

   SETUP

  To start the game, the object balls are placed in a triangular rack. The base of rack is parallel to the end rail (short end of
  the table) and positioned so the apex ball of the rack is located on the foot spot. The balls in &the rack are idealy placed so
  that they all are in the contact with one another, this is accomplished by pressing the ball &together towards the apex ball.
  The order of the ball should be rendom, with the expectation of the 8-ball, which must be placed &in the center of the rack
  (middle of the third row), and two back corner balls, one of which must be stripe and other a solid. The cue ball is placed
  anywere the breaker desires behind the head string.

   BREAK

  One person is chosen by some predetermined method (eg, coin toss, lag, win or loss of previous game) to shoot first, using
  the cue ball to break the object-ball rack apart. In most league it is the breaker's opponent who rackes the ball, but in some,
  players break thier own racks. If the breaker fails to make a successfull break-usually defined as at least four ball hitting
  cushions or an object ball being pocketed-than the opponent can opt either to play from the current position or to call for a
  re-rack or either re-break or have the orignal breaker repeat the break.

   SELECTION OF THE TARGET GROUP

  The table is "open" at the start of the game, meaning tha either player may shoot at any ball. It remains open until one
  player legally pockets any called ball other than the other 8 after the break. That playrer is assignedthe group, or suit,
  of the pocketed ball-1-7(solids),9-15 (stripes)-and other suit is assigned to the opponent. Ball pocketed on the break,
  or as the result of the foul while the table is still open, are not used to assigned the suits. Oncce the suits are assigned,
  they remain fixed throughout the game. If any balls from a player's suit are on the table, the player must hit one of them
  first on evry shoot; otherwise a foul is called and the turn ends. After all balls from the suit have been pocketed, the
  player's target becomes the 8 for the remainder of the game.

   WINNING

  A player wins the game if that palyer legally pockets the 8 ball into a designated pocket after all of thier object balls
  have been pocketed. Because of this, it is possible for a game to end with only one of the player having shot, which
  is known as "running the table" or a "denial", conversly, it's also possible to win a game without taking a shot; such
  as scenerio may occur if the opposing illegally pockets the 8 ball on any shot other than the break (such as sinking an
  8 ball in an uncalled pocket, knocking the 8 ball of the table, sinking the 8 ball when a player is not yet on the black
  ball and the cue ball off a single shot). The rules on what happens when the 8 ball is pocketed off the break vary by
  the rules in question; please read the foul section below for more information.

   FOULS

  The genral rules of pool apply to eight-ball, such as requirements that the cue ball not be pocketed and that the cushion
  be hit by any of the balls after the cue ball has stuck an object ball. Fouls specific to eight-ball are:

  ● The shooter fails to strike one of their own object balls (or the 8 ball when it is the legal ball ) with the cue ball, before
  other balls are contacted by the cue ball. This excludes "split" shots where the cue ball strikes one of the shooter's and one
  of the oponent's abject balls simultaneously.

  ● If an attempt is made to pocket a ball, and the ball hits the pocket, bounces out and lands on the ground, the ball is placed
  in the pocket and game continues.

  ● The shooter shoots the black 8 ball without designating the pocket to opposit team members or the match referee in advance.

  ● The shooter deliberately pockets the opponent's balls whik shooting the 8 ball.

  ● On the break shot, no balls are pocketed and fewer than four balls reach the cushions, in which case the incomming player
  can be demand a re-rack and take the break and force the orignal breaker to re-break, or may take ball-in -hand behind the
  head string and shoot the balls as they lie.

   BLACKBALL

  The british version eight-ball, known internationally as blackball, has involved into a seprate game, retaining significant
  elements of earlier pub versions of the game, with additional influences from english billiards and snooker. It is popular
  in amateur competition in the UK, Irland, Australia and some other countries.

  The game uses unnumbered, solid, colored object balls, typically red and yellow, with one black 8 ball. They are usually
  2 inches (51 mm) or 2+1⁄16 inches (52 mm) in a diameter, the latter being the same size as the ball used in snooker and
  English billiard. Tables for blackball pool are 6-to7-foot (1.8 to 2.1m) long, and feature pockets with rounded cushion
  openning, like snooker tables.

  The rules of blackball is differ from standard eight-ball in numreous ways, including the handing of fouls, which may
  give the opponent two shoots, racking (the 8 ball, not the apex ball, goes on the spot), selection of which group of balls
  will be shot by which player, handing of frozen balls and snookers, and many other details.

   EIGHT-BALL ROTATION

  The hybrid game eight-ball rotation is a combination of eight-ball and rotation, in which the player must pocket thier
  balls (other than 8, which remains last) in numercial order. Specifically, the solid player starts by pocketing the 1 ball
  and ascends to the 7 ball, and the stripes player starts by pocketing the 15 ball and descends to the 9 ball.

   BACKWARDS EIGHT-BALL

  A variant in which, instead of shooting cue ball at an object ball to force the object ball into a pocket, the player
  strikes the object ball with thier cue so it caroms off the cue ball and into a pocket, in a fashion similar to Russian
  pyramid. Also known as "reverse eight-ball".
